DeltaFM-VC: Contrastive flow matching with disentangled features for zero-shot voice conversion

Abstract

 Recently, research on zero-shot voice conversion (VC) based on diffusion model has been actively conducted, enabling the generation of high-quality speech. However, their reliance on multi-step denoising process results in slow inference. For real-world applications, a system capable of generating high-quality speech with a small number of steps is required. To simultaneously achieve efficiency and quality improvement, we propose a novel zero-shot voice conversion framework, DeltaFM-VC, which leverages a contrastive flow-matching based system conditioning token-level speaker embeddings. By incorporating contrastive regularization into conditional flow matching, the model generates well-separated flow trajectories, enabling high-quality speech synthesis with a small number of steps. In addition, conditioning token-level speaker embeddings helps the model adapt speaker information while suppressing unnecessary content leakage from the target speaker. These approaches work together to improve the overall quality of converted speech. Our results demonstrate that the proposed model outperforms existing approaches across multiple metrics, including naturalness, intelligibility, and speaker similarity.
